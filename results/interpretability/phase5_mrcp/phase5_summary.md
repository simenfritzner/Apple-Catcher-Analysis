# Phase 5 Summary: MRCP Analysis

## ERP Waveforms (Cz, FCz)

No classical MRCP slow negativity (Bereitschaftspotential) is present at either Cz or FCz. Instead, both channels show a broad positive deflection peaking near cue onset, with class-divergent polarity at FCz (positive for Right MI, negative for Left MI). Model importance peaks sharply around t=0 and decays post-cue at both sites, indicating the model focuses on the peri-cue transition rather than a sustained pre-movement negativity.

## Low-Frequency Saliency (0.1--5 Hz)

Pre-cue low-frequency importance (0.001068) exceeds post-cue (0.000718) by a factor of 1.49. The saliency curve peaks just before cue onset and declines monotonically into the post-cue window. The model clearly exploits slow-frequency content, but the temporal profile -- centered on cue onset rather than ramping toward movement -- is inconsistent with a classic MRCP and more consistent with a visual/anticipatory evoked potential.

## Temporal Filter MRCP Sensitivity

Five of eight learned temporal filters (F2, F3, F5, F6, F8) concentrate more than 65% of their energy below 5 Hz (peak at 0.0 Hz), qualifying as MRCP-sensitive. The remaining three filters target mu/alpha (11.7 Hz) and gamma (43.0 Hz). The dominance of low-pass/DC filters confirms the model heavily leverages sub-5 Hz content.

## Lateralized Readiness Potential

Classical LRP amplitudes show no meaningful pre/post-cue asymmetry (0.909 vs 0.896). Model lateralized saliency is small and slightly larger post-cue (0.000452) than pre-cue (0.000260). The LRP time course is noisy with no systematic lateralization pattern, indicating the model does not rely on a contralateral readiness potential for classification.

## Conclusion

The model exploits slow (<5 Hz) peri-cue activity but this activity lacks the hallmarks of a genuine MRCP: there is no pre-movement negativity at central sites, and no lateralized readiness potential. The low-frequency signal the model uses is more consistent with a stimulus-locked anticipatory or visual evoked potential than with preparatory motor cortical activity.
